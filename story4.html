The Small Candle

One stormy evening, the electricity suddenly went out. The entire house was covered in darkness. The wind howled outside, and the rain tapped loudly against the windows. The family felt uneasy as they searched for a source of light.

After a few minutes, the youngest child found a small candle in a drawer. It was thin and almost half-used. Someone lit it, and a tiny flame began to glow. Though small, it slowly pushed away the darkness and allowed everyone to see each other’s faces.

The room was not as bright as before, but the candle’s light was enough. They were able to move safely, talk comfortably, and even share stories while waiting for the power to return. The small flame brought warmth and calmness during the storm.

As they watched the candle burn, they realized something important. Even a small light can remove great darkness. It does not need to be big or powerful to make a difference.

The candle reminded everyone that even small actions — a kind word, a helping hand, or a simple smile — can bring hope in difficult times. No matter how small we think we are, we all have the power to shine.
