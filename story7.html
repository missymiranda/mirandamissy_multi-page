The Empty Jar

One day, a teacher walked into the classroom carrying a large empty jar, a box of rocks, a bag of sand, and a bottle of water. The students watched curiously, wondering what lesson she was about to teach.

First, she placed the big rocks inside the jar until it could hold no more. She then asked the class, "Is the jar full?" The students looked at it and confidently answered, "Yes."

The teacher smiled and poured sand into the jar. The sand slipped into the spaces between the rocks. She asked again, "Is it full now?" This time, the students hesitated but still said yes.

Finally, she poured water into the jar. The water filled every tiny space left between the rocks and sand. The students were surprised to see that there was still room for more.

The teacher explained that the jar represents life. The big rocks stand for the most important things — family, health, dreams, and values. The sand and water represent smaller tasks and daily distractions. If you fill your life with small things first, there will be no space for what truly matters. But when you prioritize wisely, there is always room for balance.

From that day on, the students understood that good time management and proper priorities create a meaningful and well-balanced life.
